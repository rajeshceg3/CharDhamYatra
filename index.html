Char Dham Yatra

A journey not of miles, but of transcendence. Four abodes, one path to the self, etched in the heart of the Himalayas.

Yamunotri

Source of the Yamuna River

The pilgrimage begins at the source. Here, at the westernmost shrine, the journey is one of physical effort and profound reward. The trek to the temple, nestled in a narrow gorge, is a meditation in motion.

Gangotri

Origin of the Sacred Ganga

The seat of the goddess Ganga, this is where the most revered river in Hinduism begins its epic journey. The air here is thin, but the spirit is dense, filled with the sound of the roaring river and quiet prayer.

Kedarnath

The Abode of Lord Shiva

Set against a dramatic backdrop of the highest peaks, Kedarnath is a place of primal power. Reaching this temple requires a formidable trek, mirroring the inner process of shedding the ego to stand before the divine.

Badrinath

The Seat of Lord Vishnu

The final destination is a place of serene majesty. Guarded by twin peaks, the vibrant temple of Badrinath represents the culmination of the yatra—a place of peace, clarity, and spiritual fulfillment.
